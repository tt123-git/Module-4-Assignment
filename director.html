Hayao Miyazaki

Hayao Miyazaki is a renowned Japanese film director, animator, screenwriter, and manga artist. He co-founded Studio Ghibli and is known for directing some of its most famous films, including "My Neighbor Totoro," "Princess Mononoke," "Spirited Away," and "Howl's Moving Castle." His works are praised for their imaginative stories, beautiful animation, and humanitarian themes, often featuring strong female leads. Miyazaki's contributions to the world of animation and filmmaking have earned him numerous awards and accolades.
